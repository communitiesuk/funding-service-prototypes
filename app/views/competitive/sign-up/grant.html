The Safer social housing fund is a scheme designed to test different ways of improving social housing safety and infrastructure in England. It supports local authorities, charities, and voluntary sector organisations to deal with critical housing safety issues.

Summary

Funding from the scheme can go towards a range of measures including infrastructure improvements, new safety installations, and long-term maintenance planning. The guidance sets out the aims of the fund in detail including eligibility and application details.

Eligibility

You can apply if you are:

  • Charitable incorporated organisation
  • Community benefit society or co-operative
  • Registered charity
  • Scottish charitable incorporated organisation

To take part in the scheme, the buildings you manage must meet the criteria outlined in the technical prospectus. Your grant application must have a minimum funding request value of £500.

Objectives

The main objectives of the fund are to successfully expand on the limited support available, to gather vital knowledge to support a nationwide rollout, and to ultimately increase funding accessibility to as many types of organisations as possible.

How to apply

You will need to complete the following steps to apply for this grant:

  1. Check your eligibility: Click the 'Start new application' button above and provide your email address to start the automated eligibility check and ensure your organisation is can receive MHCLG funding.
  2. Make a full application: If you meet the criteria, you will be given access to the full application form in the Access grant funding service where you can save your progress and submit your answers.
